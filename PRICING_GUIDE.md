# CaptainCTO — Pricing Reference

---

## Base price by business type

| Business | Typical scope | Price |
|---|---|---|
| Café / Bistro | Menu, hours, location, contact form | 500–650 $ |
| Salon / Spa | Services, gallery, online booking | 650–900 $ |
| Restaurant | Full menu, events page, reservation link | 700–1 000 $ |
| Boutique | Product showcase (no real e-commerce) | 700–950 $ |
| Musician / Artist | Portfolio, embedded music, show dates | 500–750 $ |
| Daycare / School | Program info, team page, registration form | 750–1 100 $ |
| Tutoring | Profile, subjects, session booking | 500–700 $ |
| Community / Church | Announcements, events, donations | 400–650 $ |

## Add-ons

| Feature | Add to base |
|---|---|
| Online booking (Calendly / Acuity) | +150–200 $ |
| Instagram feed embed | +100 $ |
| PDF menu upload + viewer | +75 $ |
| Domain + hosting setup (Netlify / Vercel) | +75–100 $ one-time |
| Rush delivery (under 5 days) | +150–200 $ |
| Extra revision round (beyond the 2 included) | +75 $ each |
| Client provides all text + photos | −75–100 $ |
| Google Maps embed + hours | Free — always include it |
| Contact / reservation form | Free — always include it |

> **Never offer real e-commerce** (cart, checkout, Shopify, WooCommerce) in this price range.
> It is a completely different scope. Minimum 2 000 $ or refer them out.

## What to charge to net a specific amount (after Stripe fees)

Stripe takes **2.9 % + 0.30 $** per transaction.

| You want to net | Charge the client |
|---|---|
| 250 $ | ~258 $ |
| 375 $ | ~387 $ |
| 500 $ | ~515 $ |
| 750 $ | ~773 $ |
| 1 000 $ | ~1 031 $ |

Round up to a clean number (520 $, 780 $, etc.) — the difference is negligible.

## How to collect payment

Use **Stripe Invoices** (not payment links, not Interac).

1. Dashboard → Invoices → + Create invoice
2. Add client name + email
3. Line item: `Acompte — Site web (50 %)` → exact amount
4. Send — client pays by card from their email
5. Repeat for the final 50 % on delivery

Each client gets a pre-set amount. No wrong entries, no chasing, full paper trail.

## Taxes

**Under 30 000 $ revenue/year:** you are a *petit fournisseur* — no GST/QST to charge or remit.

**Over 30 000 $:** register for GST/QST numbers and add 5 % + 9.975 % to every invoice.

Always set aside **25–30 %** of earnings for income tax + QPP contributions. Declare everything on your T1 (federal) and TP-1 (Quebec) each spring.

## What can be negotiated

- **Scope** — fewer pages, simpler gallery, fewer sections. Lower scope = lower price. Never lower your hourly effective rate.
- **Timeline** — more flexibility from them (2 weeks vs. 1) can justify a small discount.
- **Content** — if they provide all photos and text, take 75–100 $ off.
- **Payment schedule** — 40 % deposit / 60 % on delivery is the absolute minimum you accept. Never below 40 %.
- **Maintenance retainer** — optional for them, 50–100 $/month. Updates, photo swaps, hour changes. Pure recurring income.

---

## What cannot be negotiated

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **50 % deposit before starting** | If they won't pay it, they will ghost or become a nightmare. The deposit is also what makes them take it seriously. |
| **Trilingual included** | It is your main differentiator. A client who wants to remove it is probably not your client. |
| **Scope creep = new invoice** | If they add features mid-project, that is a change order. Say it once, clearly: *"Absolument — ça représente X heures de plus, donc un ajout de 150 $."* |
| **Your quoted rate** | Never lower the number — lower the scope instead. Keeps your perceived value intact. |

## Is it worth it for the client?

Use this to close hesitant clients.

- A salon gaining 4 extra bookings/month pays off a 750 $ site in 3–4 weeks.
- A website works 24/7. A Facebook ad campaign costs 300–500 $/month and stops the moment you stop paying.
- Being unfindable on Google means losing clients to whoever is findable.

**Strongest ROI argument for:** salons, spas, tutors, restaurants in competitive areas, Chinese-owned businesses (trilingual = solves a real pain point).

**Weaker argument for:** fully word-of-mouth businesses, very low-margin high-volume shops (depanneurs, laundromats).

## Red flags — clients to avoid or handle carefully

- Refuses the deposit → walk away
- "I'll know it when I see it" (no clear brief) → charge more or decline
- Compares you to Wix / Squarespace → explain your value or let them go
- Asks for unlimited revisions → cap it explicitly in writing before starting
- Wants e-commerce for 500 $ → re-scope or refer out
